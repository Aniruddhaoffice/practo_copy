Millions of others are also trying to adjust to these uncertain times. The good news is, there are practical and evidence-based ways to protect your mental health and prepare for what lies ahead.

Understanding the COVID-19 Resurgence: What’s Behind the Rise in Cases?

The COVID resurgence in 2025 is being driven by rising COVID cases, especially in countries like India. To keep you informed, we can look at the recent COVID cases rise in India, alongside the appearance of new strains.

The emergence of new variants, such as JN.1 and NB.1.8.1, has led to a spike in cases and reignite concerns about the virus and mental health issues caused by it.

COVID mental health challenges are compounded by the news of this resurgence, creating a sense of panic and uncertainty.

Why Is Mental Health at Risk Again?

The impact of COVID-19 on mental health is profound. As we look ahead to the COVID resurgence, many individuals may experience Post-COVID Stress Disorder, while others may see anxiety and depression.

In fact, studies from the WHO show a 25% global increase in anxiety during the pandemic, and similar patterns have been observed in 2025 by the ICMR.

These findings illustrate the emotional toll a COVID resurgence can have, especially when combined with lingering COVID mental health challenges from earlier waves.

❝ We’re seeing a resurgence in not just the virus itself, but mental health issues too. ❞

3 Best Tips to Manage COVID-19 Stress

As the COVID resurgence unfolds, there are several ways to prepare yourself mentally and emotionally. Building a strong mental foundation now can make all the difference when things start to escalate.

  • Create a Daily Routine

    A predictable schedule brings stability. Having structure during uncertain times can significantly reduce COVID anxiety.

  • Limit Doomscrolling & Take Breaks

    Constantly checking news updates can fuel COVID stress. Limit your exposure to distressing news and take breaks from social media.

  • Breathing Techniques to Calm COVID-19 Anxiety

    Simple breathing techniques, such as box breathing or the 4-7-8 method, can help you stay calm during moments of heightened anxiety.

❝ Remember, coping mechanisms or lifestyle changes are not just short-term fixes, but lifelong practices that will help you navigate COVID stress and other challenging times. ❞
— Dr. Ashish Mittal, Psychiatrist

3 Warning Signs That You Need Professional Help

Recognizing when your mental health is deteriorating is critical. If you notice any of the following warning signs, it's important to seek help from a professional.

  • Panic attacks and increased feelings of fear
  • Avoidance behavior, like refusing to leave home
  • Insomnia or disturbed sleep patterns

If you experience any of these symptoms, don’t hesitate to reach out to your nearest mental health center like Athena Behavioral Health, so you don’t have to wait for an emergency to seek help.

If you feel overwhelmed, contacting a local helpline could be the first step toward getting the support you need.

Be Aware, Not Alarmed During the COVID-19 Resurgence

It's natural to feel anxious about the COVID resurgence, but being mentally prepared can make a big difference.

While it’s important to stay informed, it’s even more crucial to focus on things that help you stay calm during the COVID rise.

By implementing coping strategies, such as limiting news intake and practicing mindfulness, you can create a sense of control.

❝ COVID mental health challenges don’t have to take over your life. Remember to stay vigilant, stay connected with others, and prioritize self-care. ❞
— Dr. Ashish Mittal, Psychiatrist

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What are the signs that indicate I should seek help for COVID-related anxiety?

Symptoms such as panic attacks, avoidance behavior, or persistent insomnia may indicate the need for professional help.

How can I stay mentally prepared for the COVID resurgence?

Practice daily routines, limit news consumption, and use breathing techniques to stay grounded.

Why is mental health at risk during a COVID resurgence?

Many individuals may experience increased anxiety or post-COVID stress disorder due to the uncertainties of the virus returning.

What are some breathing techniques I can use to cope with COVID anxiety?

Techniques like box breathing and the 4-7-8 method are proven to help reduce anxiety and bring calm.

Where can I seek mental health support during the COVID resurgence?

Many online therapy services are available, as well as local helplines. Don’t hesitate to reach out to a professional if you need support.
